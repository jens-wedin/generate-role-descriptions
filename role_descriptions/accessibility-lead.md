# Accessibility Lead

## Description
An Accessibility Lead is a leader and advocate responsible for driving the accessibility strategy and initiatives within an organization. They ensure that products, services, and digital experiences are usable by people with a wide range of disabilities. This role involves a combination of hands-on testing, team education, strategic planning, and evangelism to embed accessibility into the company's culture and processes.

## Org Level
Craft Lead

## Medium
Digital

## Industry
Tech, Government, Agency

## Responsibilities
- Develop and implement the organization's accessibility strategy and roadmap.
- Lead and mentor a team of accessibility specialists and champions.
- Establish and enforce accessibility standards and guidelines, such as WCAG.
- Conduct accessibility audits and provide recommendations for remediation.
- Collaborate with design, engineering, and product teams to integrate accessibility into the entire product development lifecycle.
- Develop and deliver accessibility training and resources to empower teams.
- Stay up-to-date with the latest accessibility standards, technologies, and best practices.
- Represent the company's commitment to accessibility to external stakeholders.

## Skills
- Expert knowledge of accessibility standards, including WCAG 2.1/2.2, Section 508, and ADA.
- Experience with a variety of assistive technologies (e.g., screen readers, screen magnifiers, voice recognition software).
- Strong leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills.
- Ability to influence and drive change across different teams and departments.
- Experience with accessibility testing tools and methodologies.
- Understanding of inclusive design principles.
- Passion for creating accessible and inclusive digital experiences.
