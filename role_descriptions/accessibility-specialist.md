# Accessibility Specialist

## Description
An Accessibility Specialist is a hands-on expert dedicated to ensuring digital products meet accessibility standards. They are responsible for testing, identifying, and providing solutions to accessibility issues. Working closely with designers and developers, they help ensure that websites, applications, and other digital content are usable by people with disabilities.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Digital

## Industry
Tech, Consulting

## Responsibilities
- Conduct thorough accessibility audits of websites and applications against standards like WCAG.
- Perform manual and automated accessibility testing using various tools and assistive technologies.
- Identify and document accessibility bugs with clear and actionable recommendations for remediation.
- Provide technical guidance and support to developers on implementing accessible code (e.g., ARIA, semantic HTML).
- Collaborate with designers to ensure UI/UX designs are accessible from the start.
- Create and maintain accessibility documentation and best practice guides.
- Participate in user research with people with disabilities.

## Skills
- Deep understanding of WCAG 2.1/2.2 and other accessibility standards.
- Proficiency with accessibility testing tools (e.g., Axe, WAVE, Lighthouse).
- Hands-on experience with assistive technologies like JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, and TalkBack.
- Strong knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, particularly in relation to accessibility.
- Familiarity with ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) and its proper implementation.
- Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills.
- Strong communication skills to effectively articulate accessibility issues and solutions.
