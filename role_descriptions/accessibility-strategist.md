# Accessibility Strategist

## Description
An Accessibility Strategist focuses on the big picture, developing and implementing long-term strategies to embed accessibility into the core of an organization's culture, processes, and products. They work at a systemic level to ensure that accessibility is not just a checklist item but a fundamental aspect of how the organization operates.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Service

## Industry
Tech, Enterprise

## Responsibilities
- Develop and champion a company-wide accessibility vision and strategy.
- Create and implement accessibility policies, standards, and governance models.
- Design and oversee the rollout of accessibility training and awareness programs across the organization.
- Establish key metrics and KPIs to measure the progress and impact of accessibility initiatives.
- Collaborate with leadership to secure buy-in and resources for accessibility.
- Identify and mitigate accessibility-related risks.
- Foster a culture of accessibility and inclusion throughout the organization.
- Engage with legal and compliance teams to ensure adherence to accessibility laws and regulations.

## Skills
- Proven experience in developing and implementing strategic initiatives.
- Strong understanding of accessibility principles and standards.
- Excellent leadership, communication, and influencing skills.
- Experience with change management and driving cultural transformation.
- Ability to work effectively with senior stakeholders and executive leadership.
- Strong analytical skills to measure the business impact of accessibility.
- Knowledge of accessibility laws and regulations.
