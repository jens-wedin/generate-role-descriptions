# Behavioural Designer

## Description
A Behavioural Designer applies principles from psychology, behavioral economics, and cognitive science to design products, services, and experiences that influence human behavior in a positive and predictable way. They seek to understand the motivations, biases, and decision-making processes of users to create solutions that help people achieve their goals.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Service

## Industry
Tech, Health, Finance

## Responsibilities
- Conduct user research to understand the psychological and behavioral drivers of user actions.
- Apply behavioral science frameworks (e.g., COM-B, Fogg Behavior Model) to analyze and map user behaviors.
- Design and prototype interventions, such as nudges, feedback loops, and incentives, to encourage desired behaviors.
- Plan and execute experiments, including A/B tests and randomized controlled trials, to measure the impact of design interventions.
- Collaborate with product managers, UX designers, and data scientists to integrate behavioral insights into the product development process.
- Communicate findings and recommendations to stakeholders in a clear and compelling way.

## Skills
- Strong understanding of behavioral science principles and frameworks.
- Proficiency in qualitative and quantitative research methods.
- Experience with experimental design and A/B testing.
- Strong analytical and critical thinking skills.
- Familiarity with UX design and product development processes.
- Excellent communication and storytelling abilities.
- A passion for understanding human behavior and creating positive change.
