# Communications Designer

## Description
A Communications Designer is a visual storyteller who translates complex ideas and messages into clear, compelling, and engaging visual content. They work at the intersection of graphic design, information design, and branding to create a wide range of materials that communicate a company's message to its target audience. They are masters of visual language, using typography, color, imagery, and layout to create a cohesive and impactful brand experience.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Digital

## Industry
Agency, Corporate

## Responsibilities
- Design and produce a wide range of communication materials, including presentations, reports, infographics, social media graphics, and marketing collateral.
- Translate complex data and information into clear and easy-to-understand visualizations.
- Ensure that all visual communications are on-brand and consistent with the company's visual identity.
- Collaborate with writers, marketers, and other stakeholders to develop and execute communication strategies.
- Manage multiple projects from concept to completion, often under tight deadlines.
- Stay current with design trends and best practices in visual communication.

## Skills
- Expertise in graphic design principles, including typography, color theory, and layout.
- Proficiency in design software such as Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign).
- Strong information design and data visualization skills.
- Excellent communication and collaboration skills.
- Ability to think strategically and translate communication goals into visual design solutions.
- Strong attention to detail and a passion for creating high-quality work.
- Experience in creating presentations and other visual storytelling assets.
