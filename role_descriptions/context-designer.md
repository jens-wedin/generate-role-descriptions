# Context Designer

## Description
A Context Designer is a specialist who focuses on understanding and designing for the broader context in which a product or service is used. They go beyond the screen to consider the user's physical, social, and emotional environment, as well as the technological and cultural factors that shape their experience. They are systems thinkers who design adaptive and responsive experiences that are tailored to the user's specific situation.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Service

## Industry
IoT, Tech

## Responsibilities
- Conduct ethnographic research and contextual inquiry to understand the user's environment and behaviors.
- Map the user's journey across different contexts, touchpoints, and channels.
- Design experiences that adapt to the user's location, time of day, and other contextual factors.
- Collaborate with designers, engineers, and data scientists to create context-aware products and services.
- Prototype and test context-aware experiences.
- Consider the ethical implications of collecting and using contextual data.

## Skills
- Expertise in qualitative research methods, such as ethnographic research and contextual inquiry.
- Strong systems thinking and service design skills.
- A deep understanding of the Internet of Things (IoT) and other emerging technologies.
- Ability to think creatively and solve complex problems.
- Strong communication and collaboration skills.
- A passion for understanding human behavior in the real world.
