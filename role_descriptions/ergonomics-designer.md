# Ergonomics Designer

## Description
An Ergonomics Designer is a specialist who applies the principles of ergonomics and human factors to the design of products, workplaces, and systems. They are focused on creating solutions that are safe, comfortable, and efficient for people to use. They are human-centered designers with a deep understanding of human anatomy, physiology, and psychology.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Physical

## Industry
Industrial, Automotive

## Responsibilities
- Conduct research and analysis to understand the physical and cognitive capabilities and limitations of users.
- Apply ergonomic principles to the design of products, workplaces, and systems.
- Create and test prototypes to evaluate the usability, safety, and comfort of designs.
- Collaborate with industrial designers, engineers, and other stakeholders to ensure that ergonomic considerations are integrated into the design process.
- Stay current with the latest research and best practices in ergonomics and human factors.
- Ensure that designs comply with relevant safety and accessibility standards.

## Skills
- A deep understanding of ergonomics and human factors principles.
- Expertise in a variety of research methods, such as task analysis, user observation, and biomechanical analysis.
- Strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
- Excellent communication and collaboration skills.
- A passion for creating safe, comfortable, and efficient products and systems.
- A degree in ergonomics, human factors, or a related field is often required.
