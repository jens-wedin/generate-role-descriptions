# Human Factors Designer

## Description
A Human Factors Designer is a specialist who applies the principles of human factors and ergonomics to the design of products, systems, and environments. They are focused on creating solutions that are safe, effective, and satisfying for people to use. They are human-centered designers with a deep understanding of human capabilities and limitations, both physical and cognitive.

## Org Level
IC

## Medium
Physical

## Industry
Medical, Industrial

## Responsibilities
- Conduct research and analysis to understand the capabilities and limitations of users in a particular context.
- Apply human factors principles to the design of products, systems, and environments.
- Create and test prototypes to evaluate the usability, safety, and effectiveness of designs.
- Collaborate with engineers, designers, and other stakeholders to ensure that human factors considerations are integrated into the design process.
- Stay current with the latest research and best practices in human factors and ergonomics.
- Ensure that designs comply with relevant safety and accessibility standards.

## Skills
- A deep understanding of human factors and ergonomics principles.
- Expertise in a variety of research methods, such as task analysis, user observation, and cognitive walkthroughs.
- Strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
- Excellent communication and collaboration skills.
- A passion for creating safe, effective, and satisfying products and systems.
- A degree in human factors, ergonomics, or a related field is often required.
